Where it tends to fit

Best suited to repeatable work that matters.

Usually customer-facing or back-office work where consistency matters and local hiring is starting to slow growth.

Customer support and service

Reliable follow-up, consistent handling, and scalable support.

Sales support and pipeline work

Outreach, lead follow-up, appointment setting, and other repeatable sales support.

Back-office and admin work

Important operational work that does not need to sit in expensive local roles.

Founder-led and operator-led businesses

Businesses that need more capability, but want a more flexible and lower-risk way to add it.

Why businesses explore it

Usually for three practical reasons.

The appeal is often straightforward: create room to grow, reduce employment drag, and add capability more flexibly.

Create room to grow

Add capability without forcing full local hiring before the business is ready.

Reduce employment drag

Lower overhead and reduce the operational weight around roles that are important but structurally repeatable.

Keep cost more predictable

Operate with clearer monthly visibility and a model that is easier to plan around.
