Where it tends to fit

Useful when the work is real, repeatable, and worth structuring properly.

This tends to suit English-speaking businesses in higher-cost labour markets that want practical offshore capability, not a heavy operating model.

Customer-facing teams

Support, customer service, follow-up, and operational coordination where consistency matters.

Sales support roles

Outbound activity, lead follow-up, appointment setting, and pipeline support that benefits from repeatable process.

Back-office work

Admin, data hygiene, case handling, and workflow support that creates drag when it sits in expensive local roles.

Growing operators

Founder-led and operator-led businesses that need more capability without taking on full local employment overhead.

Why businesses explore it

Usually for practical commercial reasons.

The appeal is not novelty. It is a cleaner operating model for the right kind of work.
